Web Accessibility

This proposal aims to establish a comprehensive strategy for implementing accessible web development practices at the Wisconsin Maritime Museum, with a particular focus on the individual proposal's emphasis on web accessibility. The primary objective is to create a digital environment that meets WCAG 2.0 or 2.1 AA standards, ensuring inclusivity for all users. The individual proposal will delve into specific accessibility challenges and propose tailored solutions to enhance the museum's digital presence. By focusing on inclusive design, ongoing accessibility monitoring, and addressing individual accessibility needs, the museum can offer an equitable and engaging experience for all visitors. This approach not only elevates the museum's commitment to accessibility but also sets a new industry standard, demonstrating the vital role of web accessibility in public engagement within the museum sector.

Technology Adaptation

    Power Dynamics in Code

    The "Technology Adoption Proposal" for the Wisconsin Maritime Museum outlines a strategic plan to modernize the museum’s operations through the adoption of web scraping and text analysis technologies. This proposal highlights the need to automate data collection and analysis processes, which are currently manual and time-consuming, thereby enhancing research capabilities, improving visitor engagement, and streamlining data management. The integration of these technologies will enable the museum to efficiently manage large volumes of historical data, provide up-to-date and interactive content for visitors, and support the museum's mission of preserving and educating about maritime history in the digital age. The proposal also addresses the challenges and ethical considerations involved, offering a comprehensive implementation plan that includes staff training, pilot testing, and ongoing evaluation to ensure the success and sustainability of the initiative.

This Proposal for the Wisconsin Maritime Museum outlines a plan to develop a tool that leverages web scraping and geocoding technologies in Python to enhance the analysis of the museum's online collection metadata. The proposal emphasizes the creation of visualizations, such as maps, to provide new insights into the geographic distribution of the museum’s photographic collection. By focusing on metadata fields like location, the tool aims to support researchers in identifying patterns and understanding the collection's strengths and weaknesses. The proposal also addresses potential biases and data inconsistencies, such as incomplete metadata fields and non-standardized dates, offering strategies to mitigate these issues. Overall, the proposal highlights the importance of using technology to create meaningful connections with the museum's historical content while ensuring accurate and equitable representation of the data..

Data Analysis

The Wisconsin Maritime Museum in Manitowoc, a key institution for preserving the history of the Great Lakes, is currently focused on digitizing its extensive collection of maritime artifacts and records. Our group’s proposal aims to enhance the museum’s digital collections database, specifically the Carus Collection, through the adoption of new technological tools and workflows. These improvements will assist researchers and archivists in better utilizing the data for purposes such as research, historical mapping, and data visualization. My individual proposal will demonstrate the use of data analytics and Python workflows, using a similar dataset from the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) as a case study. The objective is to showcase how data analysis can add narrative and context to digital collections, thereby enhancing their value and accessibility for both public patrons and researchers.
