Wisconsin Maritime Museum
Introduction:
The Wisconsin Maritime Museum is dedicated to preserving and interpreting the rich maritime
history of the Great Lakes, particularly in Wisconsin. To remain relevant in the digital age, the
museum must adopt new technologies that can enhance research capabilities, streamline data
collection, and enhance visitor engagement. This proposal advocates for the adoption of web
scraping and text analysis technologies using Python to modernize the museums operations to
align with its mission of preservation and education.
Background:
The Wisconsin Maritime Museum currently relies on traditional, manual methods for document
analysis and data collection, such as sifting through archives and physical documents. This
method is time consuming and requires more staff, limiting the museums ability to conduct
extensive and timely research. The need to manually search through historical text identification
patterns or collect information from online sources such as digital archives or social media
hinders the museums ability to generate new insights and provide up-to-date content to the
public.
Web scraping technologies can revolutionize these processes by automatically retrieving large
volumes of data from online sources. As Glez-Pea et al. (2014) point out, web scraping remains
a crucial method for data extraction, particularly when APIs are unavailable or insufficient to
meet specific data needs. This automation is especially beneficial for institutions like the
Wisconsin Maritime Museum, where the large amount of historical data requires regular updates.
Incorporating text analysis further enhances these capabilities by enabling the automated
processing and analysis of vast amounts of textual data, revealing patterns and insights that
might be missed through manual analysis. According to Qualtrics, text analysis "allows
organizations to quickly process and understand large amounts of data, turning it into actionable
insights" (para. 2). Together, these technologies can modernize the museum's research processes,
providing a more complete understanding of maritime history and improving educational
offerings.
Objectives:
The primary objectives of adopting web scraping and text analysis technologies at the Wisconsin
Maritime Museum are:
1. Enhance Research Capabilities: Automating text analysis will increase the volume and
depth of research, enabling the museum to uncover new insights into maritime history.
2. Improve Visitor Engagement: Data-driven digital content will enhance exhibits,
providing a more immersive and interactive experience for visitors.
3. Streamline Data Collection Processes: Web scraping will automate data gathering,
allowing staff to focus on strategic tasks and ensuring information is up-to-date.
4. Support the Museums Mission: Adopting these technologies will help the museum
fulfill its mission of education and preservation, maintaining a high relevance in the
digital age.
Technology Description:
Text Analysis:
Text analysis uses software to process and analyze large amounts of data by extracting patterns,
trends, and meaningful insights. In the case of the Wisconsin Maritime Museum, the technology
can be applied to a variety of projects, such as evaluating public reactions to content through
sentiment analysis.
The museum can employ sentiment analysis to enhance its understanding of how the public
responds to its content. Sentiment analysis involves examining the emotional tone of text to
determine whether it is positive, negative, or neutral. This method is particularly useful for
assessing public sentiment on social media, visitor reviews, or responses to online exhibits.

satisfaction.
As the Qualtrics article emphasizes, text analysis turns unstructured text into structured data that
can be analyzed to reveal trends, patterns, and relationships that might otherwise go unnoticed
(para. 3). This capability is particularly valuable for the museum, as it allows new insights that
can enhance research and public education to be identified.
Web Scraping:
Web scraping is the use of automated tools to extract data from websites. This technology allows
the Wisconsin Maritime Museum to automatically collect and organize information from online
sources such as historical websites. Terras et al. (2018) highlight the importance of web scraping
as a strategy to overcome the fragmentation of resources and the lack of connectivity that often
challenges large-scale digital collections in cultural heritage. This underscores the need for the
museum to adopt web scraping technologies to effectively gather and integrate various sources of
information from online sources.
The following example(Figure 3) demonstrates how web scraping can be used to extract
historical events from a Wikipedia page. The process begins by specifying the URL of the page
to scrape, in this case, a Wikipedia page related to maritime history. Using the requests library,
the script sends a request to the webpage and retrieves its HTML content. The BeautifulSoup
library is then used to parse this content, making it easier to navigate and extract specific
elements of interest.

For the Wisconsin Maritime Museum, this type of web scraping can be invaluable. Similar
scripts can be used to automatically collect historical data from various online sources, such as
maritime databases, archival websites, and educational platforms. This would significantly
reduce the time and effort required for manual data collection, enabling museum staff to focus on
preserving and interpreting information rather than collecting it.
Furthermore, by regularly running such scripts, the museum can ensure that its digital content is
always up-to-date with the latest available information. This capability is crucial for maintaining
the integrity and relevance of online exhibits and educational materials. Web scraping allows the
museum to efficiently expand its research capabilities, gather comprehensive data, and provide
visitors with rich and well-rounded historical narratives.
Web scraping can significantly reduce the time and effort required to gather and maintain digital
content, allowing the museum to have a dynamic and engaging online presence. Web scraping
enables researchers to collect real-time data from various online platforms, providing insights
into visitor preferences and behaviors that can be used to shape museum displays and programs.
By adopting this technology, the Wisconsin Maritime Museum can stay on top of modern trends
and offer exciting content that interests new audiences.
Implementation Plan:
1. Planning and Preparation: Identify specific use cases and data sources that align with
the museums goals, and engage stakeholders to ensure understanding and support.
2. Infrastructure Setup: Establish the necessary hardware and software infrastructure,
ensuring data security and compliance with ethical standards.
3. Training and Support: Provide comprehensive training for museum staff on using web
scraping and text analysis tools, and establish a support system for ongoing assistance.
4. Pilot Phase: Conduct a pilot project to test the technologies on a small scale, gather
feedback, and make necessary adjustments before full-scale implementation.
5. Full Implementation: Implement the technologies across the museums research and
digital content departments, monitoring performance and making continuous
improvements.
Challenges and Power Mitigation:
Implementing these technologies presents challenges, including initial costs, staff training, and
data privacy concerns. However, the long-term benefits outweigh these challenges. Ethical
considerations are also very important in digital humanities, the museum must address these
considerations by adopting best practices in data handling and analysis. Additionally, potential
biases in data collection can be reduced by diversifying data sources and running checks for
imbalances.
Cost-Benefit Analysis:
The initial costs of software, hardware, and training are significant but are justified by the
long-term benefits:
Increased Productivity: Automating data collection and analysis will free up staff time,
leading to more efficient use of resources and a greater output of high-quality research
and content.
Enhanced Research Capabilities: The museum will be able to conduct more in-depth
research, contributing to its reputation as a leading institution in maritime history.
Improved Visitor Engagement: Richer, data-driven content will attract and retain more
visitors, both online and in person.
Long-Term Savings: The automation of data collection processes will lead to significant
cost savings over time.
The findings of Terras et al. (2018) highlights the long-term benefits of investing in research
infrastructure and training, noting that such investments not only enhance research capabilities
but also ensure effective management and analysis of large-scale data. These findings further
support the financial viability of the museum's proposed technology adoption and emphasize the
strategic value and impact of these long term investments.
Evaluation and Metrics:
To measure the effectiveness of these technology implementations, the museum should track the
following:
1. Number of Documents Analyzed: Track the volume of documents processed and new
insights generated.
2. Visitor Engagement: Monitor engagement with digital content, using metrics such as
website traffic and visitor feedback.
3. Time and Cost Savings: Evaluate the efficiency gains from automating data collection
and analysis processes.
4. Staff Feedback: Gather feedback from staff on the usefulness and effectiveness of the
tools.
5. Research Output: Measure the increase in research output, such as the number of new
collaborations resulted from using these technologies.
Conclusion:
The adoption of web scraping and text analysis technologies at the Wisconsin Maritime Museum
represents a strategic investment that will modernize research processes, enhance digital content,
and strengthen the museums task of preserving maritime history. While the initial investment
may be substantial, the long-term benefits such as increased research capabilities, improved
visitor engagement, and significant time and cost savings will ensure that the museum remains at
the forefront of digital humanities.
By implementing these technologies, the museum will not only enhance its current operations
but also position itself to seize future opportunities in the rapidly evolving digital landscape. This
initiative will help the museum thrive in the digital age by maintaining its relevance and impact
in the field of maritime history research and education.
